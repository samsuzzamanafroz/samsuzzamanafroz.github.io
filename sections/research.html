Research Interests

My research spans theoretical and observational astrophysics, with a focus on fundamental physics and tests of general relativity, as well as the development of innovative statistical and computational methods for gravitational-wave astronomy and cosmology.

Gravitational Wave Astronomy

Analyzing LIGO–Virgo–KAGRA data to understand binary black hole populations, formation channels, and the evolutionary history of compact objects in the universe.

Tests of General Relativity

Performing parametric and non-parametric tests of gravity in both weak- and strong-field regimes using gravitational-wave observations from current and future detectors.

Learn more →

Gravitational Wave Bursts

Studying hyperbolic encounters of compact objects as gravitational-wave sources to probe properties of dark matter.

Learn more →

Dark Matter using Gravitational Waves

Investigating potential signatures of dark matter profile and exotic compact objects through gravitational-wave observations.

Learn more →

Multi-Messenger Cosmology

Integrating gravitational wave observations with electromagnetic surveys to probe cosmological parameters with unprecedented precision.

Learn more →

Phase Space Analysis

Designing fast, high-dimensional trajectory analysis pipelines to classify black hole formation scenarios and extract physical parameters directly from observational data.
