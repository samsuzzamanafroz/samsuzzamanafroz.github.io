About Me

Ph.D. candidate at Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), Mumbai working at the intersection of fundamental physics, astrophysics, and cosmology with gravitational waves.

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), Mumbai, where my research focuses on fundamental physics, astrophysics, and cosmology using gravitational-wave observations. My work lies at the interface of theory, data analysis, and statistical inference, with a strong emphasis on extracting robust physical constraints from complex and noisy datasets.

My research includes cosmological parameter estimation, tests of gravity in both the weak- and strong-field regimes, and studies of the astrophysical population of compact binary mergers, particularly binary black holes. I work with data from current and future gravitational-wave detectors, including LIGO–Virgo–KAGRA, LISA, Cosmic Explorer, and the Einstein Telescope, and combine these with electromagnetic cosmological probes such as DESI and the Pantheon supernova sample.

Methodologically, I develop and apply advanced Bayesian inference techniques, including hierarchical modeling and likelihood-based analyses, to high-dimensional observational data. As a member of the LIGO–Virgo–KAGRA Collaboration, I contribute to gravitational-wave data analysis and cosmological inference efforts within large international collaborations.

More broadly, my work is motivated by the use of rigorous statistical methods to address fundamental questions in physics and cosmology, and I am interested in extending these quantitative approaches to a wide range of data-intensive scientific problems.
